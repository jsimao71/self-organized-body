# Evidence Map — Version 0.2

| Proposition | Status | Strongest support | Main gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organisms preserve function through material turnover | Established | cell/protein turnover, homeostasis, memory systems | degree to which “organization” can be measured across all levels |
| No biological level is universally causally privileged | Established framework | Noble, Ellis, systems biology | operationalization in specific interventions |
| Higher-level projects alter lower-level biology | Established in ordinary causal chains | stress, behaviour, exercise, allostasis | magnitude and specificity of deep rejuvenation effects |
| Relaxation releases motor degrees of freedom | Supported | motor abundance, reduced co-contraction, exploratory learning | relation to long-term structural rebuilding |
| Healthy form behaves as an attractor family | Supported in development; broader hypothesis in adults | developmental robustness, morphogenesis | adult convergence after major damage |
| Mature cell identity is not always irreversible | Established but tissue-specific | dedifferentiation, transdifferentiation, reprogramming | safe endogenous access and functional integration |
| Classical progenitor exhaustion is not a universal impossibility proof | Conceptually supported | mature-cell plasticity and axon regrowth | practical pathway in each tissue |
| Functional restoration may use approximate micro-mechanisms | Established in compensation; broader hypothesis in regeneration | neural degeneracy, collateral pathways | fidelity for precision organs |
| Systemic progressive loading expands adaptive bandwidth | Plausible synthesis | training, learning, stress inoculation | integrated longitudinal human studies |
| Aging contains reinforcing deterioration loops | Strongly supported | frailty, inflammaging, mitochondrial and immune interactions | exact dynamical form and reversibility |
| Improvement can become self-reinforcing | Plausible | exercise-function feedback, rehabilitation | evidence for sustained organism-wide positive runway |
| Endogenous maintenance can indefinitely outrun aging | Speculative | no direct human evidence | long-term multi-hallmark reversal |
| Biological age is better represented as a distribution | Conceptual proposal supported by heterogeneity | tissue-specific clocks and functions | formal definition and validation |
| Parenthood reactivates multiple age-associated modes | Plausible behaviourally | play, attachment, motor and hormonal changes | cellular rejuvenation claim unsupported |
| The described practice regenerates a damaged human optic nerve | Unsupported | relevant mouse reprogramming only | direct clinical evidence |
